Our News and Information

Sola Gratia School

Sola Gratia School held a lively Lunar New Year celebration on Monday, February 3, 2025. The event showcased Chinese cultural traditions, bringing the school community together to appreciate Indonesia's diverse cultural heritage. The celebration began with a traditional lion dance performance in the school courtyard, followed by the sound of drums and cymbals echoing throughout the area. Students and teachers watched in awe as the lion dancers moved rhythmically, believed to bring good fortune and drive away negative energy. Inside the school hall, students from various grades presented Chinese songs, calligraphy, and a short drama illustrating the legend of Nian, the mythical creature associated with Lunar New Year. During the event, one of the students, Kevin from grade VIII, said, “I'm not Chinese, but I love learning about this tradition. It's colorful and meaningful.” His friend, Alicia, added, “Celebrating together makes us feel closer, no matter our background.” Principal Maria Lestari expressed her gratitude in her closing remarks: “Events like this help students respect differences and celebrate values like family, gratitude, and renewal—things we all share.” The celebration ended with the symbolic giving of angpao envelopes containing words of blessing, shared from teachers to students. Though symbolic, it brought smiles and joy to many. Through the Lunar New Year celebration, Sola Gratia continues to nurture an inclusive and respectful learning environment, where every student feels seen, valued, and inspired to understand the world around them.
