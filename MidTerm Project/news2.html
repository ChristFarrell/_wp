Our News and Information

Sola Gratia School

Sola Gratia School was filled with joy and celebration on Saturday, March 22, 2025. The school, established in 2008, marked its 30th anniversary with the theme “Growing in Faith, Excellence, and Service.” The celebration took place from morning until afternoon and involved students, teachers, staff, parents, and alumni. The event began with a thanksgiving service led by Pastor Yohanes Sitorus. In his short sermon, he emphasized the importance of gratitude for the school's journey and the need to keep growing in love and responsibility as a Christian education community. After the service, the cultural parade took place. Students from each class represented a region of Indonesia through costumes, dances, songs, and cultural presentations. Loud cheers filled the schoolyard when the 11th graders performed the Saman dance from Aceh energetically and in perfect sync. Melinda, a 10th-grade student, shared, “At first I was nervous, but after weeks of practice and support from my friends, I gained confidence. It was so much fun!” The entertainment stage followed, showcasing musical drama, poetry, a teacher choir, and a student band. This was also the moment for launching Sola Gratia's digital yearbook, featuring the school's milestones over the years.At 09:30 a.m., guests were invited to enjoy the food bazaar and student project exhibitions. Food stalls managed by parents offered dishes like pempek, gudeg, siomay, and traditional cakes. There were also booths displaying arts and crafts created by students.During the afternoon session, the highlight was the ceremonial cutting of the birthday tumpeng by the Principal, Mrs. Maria Lestari, accompanied by student representatives and senior teachers. In her speech, she expressed her deep gratitude for the school's growth over the past 30 years.

“It's not easy to build a strong and character-driven learning community. But by God's grace and with everyone's support, we've made progress. May Sola Gratia continue to be a light and a blessing,” she said to the audience. The event concluded with a balloon release and a special performance by alumni from the first graduating class, who sang an original song titled “Untukmu, Sola Gratia.” The song reflected their fond memories and hopes for the school's continued impact. The 30th anniversary celebration was not just a commemoration, but also a reflection and inspiration to keep growing. Sola Gratia remains committed to providing high-quality education, building character, and meeting future challenges with a spirit of service.
