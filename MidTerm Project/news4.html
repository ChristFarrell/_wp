Our News and Information

Sola Gratia School

Sola Gratia School once again carved out a valuable moment through a campus visit activity held on Tuesday, April 29, 2025. This activity involved students in grades XI and XII to get to know the world of higher education directly. Taking place at Universitas Nasional Nusantara, this visit aims to broaden students' horizons regarding college life and various paths to further education after graduating from high school. The activity began at 09.00 am with a warm welcome by the campus Public Relations team. The students were then invited to take a campus tour that included visits to the library, laboratory, classrooms, and student dormitories. The impression of enthusiasm was clearly seen from the faces of the students who actively asked questions and documented their experiences. In the question and answer session, a student named Rafi asked the campus supervisor, "Is the scholarship program here open to students from private schools like ours?" This question was immediately greeted with enthusiasm by the campus. "Of course. We open equal opportunities for all students, both from public and private schools. As long as they meet the criteria, everyone can compete fairly," answered Mrs. Ratna, the representative for the academic field.

The visit was also interspersed with a short seminar on choosing a major and strategies for successfully entering college. The resource person conveyed the importance of knowing one's potential, interests, and career prospects before making a choice. Mrs. Maria Lestari, Principal of Sola Gratia, stated that this campus visit was part of the student self-development program that is routinely held every year. "We want students to not only excel in their studies, but also be ready to make big decisions in their lives. By seeing the campus environment directly, they can imagine the future and prepare themselves better," she said. The students were also invited to discuss directly with active students from various majors. This interaction provided a real picture of college life, both from an academic and social perspective. The activity ended with a group photo session and the presentation of an award plaque from the school to the university as a symbol of gratitude. This visit is expected to motivate students to study harder and plan their future from an early age. With the spirit of learning and exploration, Sola Gratia continues to be committed to providing students with relevant, fun, and educational experiences.
